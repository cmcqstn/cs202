← Sights of WWU Stone Enclosure: Rock Rings

Completed and placed on Western's campus by Nancy Holt in 1978, Stone Enclosure: Rock Rings sits in the southeast corner of campus between the Academic Instructional Center and the Fairhaven dorms.

Rock Rings is made of a type of schist called Brown Mountain Stone, mined in Harrison Hot Springs, BC. Each stone has an uneven, unfinished surface which contrasts with the sophistication of the concentric rings in which it is arrayed. Stonemason Al Poynter, who assisted in its construction, used little mortar in order to emphasize the irregularity of the stones.

In contrast to the rest of Western's sculpture collection, which is dominated by abstract and metal works, Rock Rings is refreshingly simple. It conjures the image of an ancient observatory, and provides a sense of peace and safety through natural barriers of stone and mortar.
